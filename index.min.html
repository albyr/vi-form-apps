Apps for VI Formers

Mr Reid's Favourites

Google Keep

Google Keep is a powerful notetaking / "to do" list-keeping app that integrates with your Google account. Notes can be taken as images, sound files or as text (including checklists). It also has a powerful location-based reminder system; this is very useful if you need to reminded of something at a particular place, rather than at a particular time.

Download: Google Play, Chrome Web Store

Pocket

Pocket is a read-it-later service. If you come across an interesting article but don't have time to read it, just click to add it your Pocket and it will be downloaded to your device for offline reading. Pocket also strips out adverts, comments, etc for a much better reading experience, and keeps track of articles you've marked as read so that you can always find that article you liked.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: Instapaper, Readability, Evernote Clearly

Feedly

Feedly is an RSS aggregator. This means that you can get updates from all the sites you check regularly in one place, without having to visit each site individually. To read your items you can use the Feedly app itself, or one of the other clients if you prefer.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: Digg Reader, Flipboard, Feedbin

Glympse

Glympse is an app for secure realtime location sharing. When you send a Glympse to someone, they're then able to see where you are for as long as you specify. Glympse is a very useful tool for letting people know that you're on your way and when you're going to arrive, and you can create public groups that multiple people can use to see each other's locations, very useful if a number of you have agreed to meet somewhere.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Productivity

 

Evernote

Evernote is a very powerful and very widely used notetaking app. It features handwriting recognition and OCR, and includes powerful tagging and search features. Evernote also offer a selection of other productivity tools.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Wunderlist

Wunderlist for Education

Wunderlist for Education is a productivity and task management app specially designed for school pupils. It includes collaboration features that are useful when working on group projects, organising parties, etc.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Clear

Clear is a beautiful and minimal productivity and task management app that integrates with iCloud. It is also available as a standalone Mac desktop app.

Download: iTunes

Remember the Milk

Remember the Milk is a popular productivity and task management app that integrates seamlessly with lots of other services and apps, such as Evernote, Gmail, Outlook, Twitter, etc.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Studying and Revision

 

Duolingo

Duolingo is a free, fun and interactive way to learn and practice a foreign language. It includes support for Spanish, French, Italian, German and Portuguese and its approach has been validated by a scientific study.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: Babbel

Anki

Anki is a piece of spaced repetition flashcard software. Flashcards can include text, images, sounds and/or equations, and Anki learns what you do and don't know so that you don't have to practice material you've mastered. Different clients are available for different operating systems.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

StudyBlue

StudyBlue is another flashcard app. It isn't as powerful as Anki, but it's more polished and a bit easier to use, and has a very extensive library of pre-made flashcards.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Backup & Collaboration

 

Google Drive

Google Drive

Google Drive and Docs

Google Drive offers 15 GB of storage that syncs automatically with a directory on your computer, very useful for automated backups. It also gives you access to Google Docs, allowing you to edit a document and track changes to it from any computer with a web browser. Google Docs also includes powerful sharing and collaboration features, which make it easy to work together on group projects.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: SkyDrive

Dropbox

Dropbox is an extremely popular online cloud storage, backup and file-hosting app. Like Google Drive, it syncs with a directory on your computer, making automated backups easy, and it can be configured to automatically store photographs taken on a phone or tablet. New users get 2 GB of storage for free (though there are various tricks you can use to get more free space), and 100 GB can be had for less than Â£10 per month.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: SkyDrive

Flickr

Flickr is the leading photo-sharing website, and offers an astonishing 1000 GB of storage for photographs and short videos. It also features social networking features, so that you can follow your friends and like/comment on their photographs, and powerful search/tagging features.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: Picasa

Miscellaneous

YouTube Remote

YouTube Remote allows you to use your phone or tablet as a remote control for YouTube videos running on a desktop computer, smart TV or games console. Anyone with the sharing code can add videos, so it's great for creating shared playlists for parties, etc.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Citymapper

Citymapper is the best way to get around London by public transport, including by tube and Boris Bike. Alternative versions are also available for New York City, Paris and Berlin.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Cover

Cover is an alternative lockscreen for Android devices that uses your location and the time of day to learn when and where you use different apps, and then puts those apps directly onto your lockscreen for easy access.

Download: Google Play

Wolfram|Alpha

Wolfram|Alpha is an incredibly powerful factual "knowledge engine" that can perform all sorts of calculations, conversions and much more.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

PocketCasts

PocketCasts is an audio and video podcast catcher and player. It has a beautiful interface and powerful search functions, and syncs across all types of devices — you can start listening to a podcast on your iPhone and finishing listening to it on your Android tablet seamlessly.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

Last.fm

Last.fm does some of the same things as a service like Spotify or Rdio, but its main selling point is its "scrobbling" function, which monitors the music you play through any app on your device and recommends other music you might like.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

See also: Spotify, Rdio

YPlan

YPlan presents a curated list of events in London with cheap tickets available. Payment is done through the app, and no printing of tickets is required - everything is electronic. There is also a version of YPlan for New York City.

Download: Google Play, iTunes

IFTTT

IFTTT ("If This Then That") is a very powerful app and website that helps tie together various other internet services. You can set it to e-mail you when someone tags you on Facebook, or automatically add photographs from Instagram to your Dropbox, etc. The possibilities are almost endless.

Download: iTunes
